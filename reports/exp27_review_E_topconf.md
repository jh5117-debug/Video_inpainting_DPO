# Exp27 Review E: Top-Conference Reviewer Perspective

## Recommendation

**Current likely verdict: Reject / borderline weak reject unless the paper is reframed and supported by much stronger evidence.**

From a strict CVPR/ICCV perspective, the obvious concern is that Exp27 can collapse into a straightforward composition of already public ideas:

- **LocalDPO applied to video inpainting:** real/ground-truth video as winner, model-corrupted local region as loser, region-aware DPO on the mask.
- **SDPO plus an inpainting region mask:** winner-preserving loser-gradient scaling, but with the reconstruction loss computed on the hole/boundary region.
- **Linear-DPO plus a better dataset:** sigmoid utility replaced by clipped linear utility and an EMA reference, trained on inpainting preference pairs.

If this is all the paper claims, it is not enough for CVPR/ICCV. LocalDPO already owns the central localized preference construction and mask-aware DPO idea for video diffusion. Diffusion-SDPO already owns the safe loser-gradient scaling rule. Linear-DPO already owns the linear utility and EMA reference. A paper that simply ports these mechanisms to video inpainting would read as a competent engineering application, not a top-conference contribution.

## Prior-Art Boundary

**LocalDPO is the closest and most dangerous prior.** The paper explicitly constructs winner/loser pairs from real videos and locally corrupted versions generated by the base VDM. Its code implements mask-weighted winner/loser MSE, mask DPO, full DPO, and SFT terms. This is almost the same abstraction one would naturally use for inpainting: the only difference is that inpainting has user/task masks rather than random Bezier masks.

**Diffusion-SDPO is orthogonal, not a new inpainting contribution by itself.** The official code computes an output-space safe lambda from winner/loser residual gradients and uses it only to scale the loser branch. Applying that scale to a masked inpainting DPO loss is a plausible stabilizer, but the intellectual contribution remains SDPO unless Exp27 derives and validates a genuinely different partial-observation or mask-coupled guarantee.

**Linear-DPO is also orthogonal.** The official code implements a clipped linear ratio, `0.2 * beta * (model_diff - ref_diff) + 0.5`, and optionally updates the reference by EMA. Using this on a new inpainting preference dataset is not a method contribution unless the paper shows that inpainting has a distinct preference geometry that requires a modified utility, reference policy, or data construction.

## Contribution Triage

| Candidate contribution | Reviewer E decision | Reason |
|---|---:|---|
| "We apply LocalDPO to video inpainting" | **Reject** | Too direct. LocalDPO already uses real positives, local model corruptions, masks, and region-aware DPO. |
| "We use SDPO on masked inpainting losses" | **Reject as main contribution** | Useful stabilizer, but the safe-update idea is prior work. Needs to be an ablation/component, not the claimed novelty. |
| "We use Linear-DPO on an inpainting preference dataset" | **Reject as main contribution** | Dataset plus known objective is not enough without a nontrivial task-specific construction or broad evidence. |
| "We build object-removal-specific preference pairs that prevent outside-mask shortcuts and target hole/boundary realism" | **Potentially acceptable** | This could be a real task contribution if the construction is demonstrably different from LocalDPO and solves a video inpainting-specific failure mode. |
| "We propose a mask/boundary/background decomposed preference objective with anti-shortcut compositing and temporal consistency terms" | **Potentially acceptable** | Acceptable only if it is more than weighted MSE and is supported by ablations against LocalDPO-style mask-only DPO. |
| "We provide a rigorous inpainting benchmark and evidence protocol for preference optimization" | **Potentially acceptable as secondary contribution** | Could matter if it includes strong baselines, blind human study, temporal metrics, data leakage controls, and cross-model validation. |

## What Could Be Accepted

The strongest acceptable paper is not "DPO for inpainting." It is:

**A video-inpainting-specific preference optimization framework that constructs controlled winner/loser pairs around task masks, prevents preference shortcuts outside the hole, explicitly models hole/boundary/background tradeoffs, and proves through strong ablations that generic LocalDPO/SDPO/Linear-DPO are insufficient.**

The paper needs to make the inpainting task do real conceptual work. For example:

1. **Preference construction:** The loser should be generated under the actual object-removal/inpainting mask, then composited so that differences outside the intended region cannot become a trivial preference cue. This is a meaningful distinction from random LocalDPO corruption only if demonstrated.
2. **Objective:** The loss should separately treat hole interior, boundary ring, near-background, and temporal consistency. A mere binary mask-weighted DPO loss is too close to LocalDPO.
3. **Task-specific failure modes:** Evidence must show improvements on object disappearance, boundary blending, background consistency, temporal flicker, hallucinated remnants, and mask leakage.
4. **Cross-model generality:** Results should hold on at least two inpainting backbones or adapter settings, not only one DiffuEraser-style pipeline.
5. **Direct prior comparison:** The paper must include a faithful LocalDPO-style inpainting baseline, plus SDPO-only and Linear-DPO-only masked variants.

## What Should Be Rejected

I would recommend rejection for any paper whose core claim is one of the following:

- "We replace LocalDPO random masks with inpainting masks."
- "We add SDPO safe lambda to region-weighted DPO."
- "We train Linear-DPO on our inpainting pairs."
- "We improve DAVIS metrics with a new preference dataset, but do not compare to LocalDPO adapted to inpainting."
- "We use real videos as winners and model outputs as losers, without controlling outside-mask artifacts."

These are not top-conference-level claims. They are reasonable implementation variants.

## Required Evidence For Strong Accept

For a strong accept, I would need the following minimum evidence.

1. **Faithful baselines**

- Base inpainting model.
- SFT on the same winners.
- Vanilla DPO on global reconstruction loss.
- LocalDPO-style masked corruption adapted to inpainting.
- Region-only DPO without SDPO/Linear-DPO.
- SDPO-only masked DPO.
- Linear-DPO-only masked DPO.
- Full proposed method.
- At least one strong non-DPO inpainting baseline such as the project’s best existing adapter or a current official video inpainting model.

2. **Ablations that isolate novelty**

- Real winner + generated loser vs LocalDPO random local corruption.
- Composited loser vs raw generated loser.
- Hole-only vs boundary-only vs hole+boundary+background decomposition.
- Full-frame DPO vs masked DPO.
- Mask scale, mask morphology, and partial/full mask variants.
- SDPO lambda on/off.
- Linear utility on/off.
- EMA reference on/off.
- SFT regularization on/off.
- Temporal consistency term on/off, if claimed.

3. **Metrics that match inpainting**

- Hole-region LPIPS/PSNR/SSIM or equivalent reconstruction metrics where ground truth exists.
- Boundary-ring perceptual and color consistency metrics.
- Outside-mask preservation metrics to ensure the method is not changing easy regions.
- Temporal warping/flicker metrics in hole and boundary regions.
- Object-removal-specific metrics: residual object detector score, mask leakage, background completion plausibility.
- Blind human study focused on inpainting quality, not generic visual preference.

4. **Evaluation design**

- DAVIS and YouTube-VOS are not enough unless split hygiene is explicit.
- Use held-out masks and held-out video categories.
- Include large-motion, thin-structure, transparent-object, camera-motion, and dynamic-background cases.
- Report failure cases, not only cherry-picked grids.
- Keep VOR-Eval or any final benchmark strictly final-only, not used for model choice.

5. **Robustness and training evidence**

- Multiple seeds or confidence intervals.
- Equal compute and equal data comparisons.
- Training curves showing whether winner loss, loser loss, and masked margin behave as claimed.
- Evidence that gains are not from shortcut artifacts outside the mask.
- Cross-backbone validation.

## Hard Rejection Text

The paper proposes a video inpainting preference optimization method, but the technical novelty is not sufficiently distinguished from concurrent and prior diffusion preference optimization work. The central data construction, using real videos as preferred samples and locally degraded/model-generated regions as dispreferred samples, is already the core contribution of LocalDPO. The proposed region/mask-weighted DPO objective is likewise a direct specialization of LocalDPO’s region-aware DPO to an inpainting mask. The safety and utility variants are inherited from Diffusion-SDPO and Linear-DPO, respectively, without a new derivation or empirical finding specific enough to video inpainting. As a result, the submission reads primarily as an application and combination of existing DPO variants rather than a new method.

The experimental evidence also does not yet meet the bar needed to overcome this novelty concern. A convincing submission would need direct comparisons to faithful LocalDPO-for-inpainting, SDPO-only, Linear-DPO-only, and region-DPO baselines under identical data and compute, plus inpainting-specific metrics that isolate hole realism, boundary blending, outside-mask preservation, and temporal consistency. Without these controls, the reported gains cannot be attributed to the claimed contribution rather than to known preference losses, dataset construction, or evaluation artifacts. I therefore recommend rejection.

## Accept Conditions

I would move toward weak accept or accept only if the final paper satisfies all of the following:

1. The method is explicitly positioned as **not** LocalDPO, SDPO, or Linear-DPO, and the paper states exactly which components are borrowed.
2. The genuinely new component is an inpainting-specific preference construction or objective that LocalDPO does not already imply.
3. A faithful LocalDPO-adapted inpainting baseline is implemented and beaten.
4. SDPO and Linear-DPO are treated as optional stabilizers/ablations unless the paper provides new inpainting-specific theory.
5. The main gains survive equal-data, equal-compute, multi-seed, cross-backbone comparisons.
6. The evaluation proves improvements inside the mask and at the boundary while preserving outside-mask content.
7. Human evaluation is blind, inpainting-specific, and includes failure-mode categories.
8. The paper includes negative results and clearly reports cases where preference optimization harms fidelity or temporal stability.

## Final Reviewer E Position

The acceptable contribution is **not** "preference optimization for video inpainting" in the broad sense. That space is already heavily covered by LocalDPO-style localized preference data and by SDPO/Linear-DPO objective variants. The acceptable contribution would be a sharply defined, inpainting-native preference framework that solves a real gap: preventing shortcut preference signals, improving hole/boundary temporal realism, and showing that generic localized DPO does not suffice.

Until that evidence exists, I would reject the work as an incremental combination of LocalDPO + region mask + SDPO/Linear-DPO components.
